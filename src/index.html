Writing Samples

Shiba Inu

My most recent work is on Shiba Inu Documentation Webpage. Shiba Inu is a company that started from a Memecoin and later became a provider of different blockchain solutions, such as an L2, a Swap service and even Web3 games. For all those products, documentation is needed and that is why I was hired. We created those docs from scratch, comprising overall specifications, technical and non-technical tutorials.

Klever

Klever is a very disruptive Web3 company whose employees are mostly very creative Brazilian individuals. Their flagship products are a crypto wallet, a crypto exchange and a blockchain.

As the first and only technical writer in the company, I was responsible for the entire documentation, internal and external (Klever documentation), which involved API references and tutorials. I'd also take care of the company's knowledge management and the accuracy of the applications' microcopy - Ux writing.

Here are a few samples of my work at Klever:

  • An article about Klever's crytpo wallet newest version.
  • A series of articles with information about the Klever blockchain specs.
  • Another series of articles with instructions to interact with the Klever blockchain.

Polygon Labs

Polygon Labs is a behemoth in the crypto space. It basically offers solutions to the Ethereum lingering scalability issues through a number of Layer 2 alternatives.

At Polygon Labs, I was part of an ever-changing Documentation team, that was directly responsible for the Wiki, the company's very comprehensive and extensive external documentation. There, I would write, edit and manage technical articles for developers and researchers, as well as, test and produce instructional guides for end-users.

Here are a few samples of my work at Polygon Labs:

  • A tutorial about the Polygon Wallet suite for regular end-users.
  • A list of reviewed and edited tutorials provided by third-party companies.
  • A Style Guide comprised of Writing Guidelines and Tutorial Templates to set the standards for external contributors.

Polygon ID

Polygon ID, now known as Privado ID, is a division of the Polygon company specialized in the Self-Soverign Identity business. I was the directly responsible individual for the ID's documentation, which meant that I had to vet all updates for the docs before they went live. I also managed the creation, management and deployment of this docs/developer hub.

Here are a few samples of my work with the Polygon ID team:

  • A Guide to the Credential Marketplace.

About Me

I'm based in Rio de Janeiro. I hold a degree in Languages- Portuguese and English and am also pursuing a degree in Computer Systems. As a natural sequence from my graduation in Linguistics in 2008, I started my professional career as a high school and language institute teacher and tutor. I was driven to that area for an interest in how languages work and how pedagogical theories could be used to foster students learning. I worked for important language courses and a few traditional schools in Rio de Janeiro.

Although I can't stress enough how important teachers are for the world and how fulfilling that career could be for a lot of illuminated people, I wasn't too happy in the classroom.

I decided to pursue an old and persistent interest in technology, which would date back to my teenage years when I learned to assemble and configure computer pieces, a very valuable skill before the 2000s. I did a few courses, went back to university, and even became an intern again at the age of 30 years old.

I mixed all those incredible experiences together, balanced the best of each field and my own personal interests to decide that Technical Writing is my ideal career. Tech writers' skills combine all those interests I've always had in a practical and resourceful manner. It involves a deep knowledge of technology, which entangles coding, infrastructure, data analysis and other exciting fields; but at the same time it is deeply rooted in language and education, as it advocates the use of a clear and concise discourse to convey precise instructions and product knowledge.
